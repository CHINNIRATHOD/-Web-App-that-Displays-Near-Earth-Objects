THE SUN ☀

The name Sun It comes from the Latin "sun", used by the ancient Romans to refer to the star that illuminated the day and was seen as a symbol of vitality and power. Since time immemorial, this majestic star has been an object of fascination, guiding life on Earth beneath its warm glow. Their tireless energy has allowed life to thrive, providing us with light and warmth throughout the centuries..

CHARACTERISTICS

  • It is classified as a spectral type star G2 and belongs to the luminosity classV,which is represented as a "G2V" star.
  • The diameter of the Sun is approximately1.4 million kilometers, although this measurement may vary slightly due to fluctuations in solar activity. It is important to note that its diameter is approximately 109 times larger than that of the Earth.
  • The distance from the center of the Sun to its surface, that is, its radius, is approximately 696,340 kilometers. It is a large star compared to the planets in our solar system.
  • The age of the Sun is approximately 4.5 billion years.
  • The approximate composition of the Sun is around 74% hydrogen and about 24% helium, with the rest made up of elements in small quantities, such as oxygen , carbon, neon, nitrogen, magnesium and iron.

CURIOSITIES

  • Solar cycles: The Sun goes through an activity cycle of approximately 11 years, marked by fluctuations in the number of sunspots, flares and coronal mass ejections.< /li>
  • Extreme temperatures: The temperature on the solar surface is around 5,778 degrees Kelvin, while in its core, where nuclear fusion occurs, 15 million degrees Kelvin.
  • Powerful gravity: The Sun's gravity is one of the factors that keeps the solar system in balance, with a gravitational acceleration of 274 m/s² on its surface.
  • Solar energy: In addition to being vital for life, the Sun is the main source of renewable energy on Earth. Through solar panel technology, we can harness its energy in a sustainable way.
