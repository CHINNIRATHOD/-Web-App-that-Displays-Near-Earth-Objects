SATURN

The name Saturn comes from the Roman god Saturn, who was considered the god of agriculture and harvest. Saturn was one of the most important gods in Roman mythology, and his influence It spread to the daily life of the Romans in fields such as religion, politics and the economy.

CHARACTERISTICS

  • It is one of the few planets that has rings around it.
  • According to a study in Leicester, it was concluded that there is water on Saturn in various forms of its composition.
  • Gravity on Saturn is 10.4 m/s², this is approximately 10.4 times the acceleration due to gravity on the Earth's surface.
  • The atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Its atmosphere It extends to a great depth and is made up of several layers.
  • Clouds, storms, auroras and winds are also found in Saturn's atmosphere. up to 1800 km/h.

CURIOSITIES

  • It is the 6th planet in order of distance from the sun.
  • A day on Saturn lasts about 11 hours, where 10 hours are day and 1 hour are night.
  • The transfer has a duration of 29 years.
  • The rotation lasts 11 hours.
  • Its radius measures around 58,000 km.
  • Saturn does not have a defined solid surface like the other terrestrial planets.
