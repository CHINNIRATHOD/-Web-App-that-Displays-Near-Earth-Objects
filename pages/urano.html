URANUS

From Latin Uranus, from the Latin translation of ancient Greek representative of the sky and father of the god Saturn.

It is known for its extreme inclination, since its axis of rotation is inclined more than 90° in relation to its orbital plane, which means it appears to be turning on its side.

CHARACTERISTICS

  • AVERAGE TEMPERATURE → -218° C.
  • PRESENCE OF WATER? → Yes, mostly ice.
  • COMPOSITION OF THE ATMOSPHERE → Hydrogen, helium, methane, water and ammonia.
  • GRAVITY → 8.7 m/s²
  • SURFACE → 8,083 billion km².
  • LENGTH OF THE DAY → 17H 14' 24''.
  • DURATION OF ONE YEAR → 84 Earth years.

CURIOSIDADES

  • Very tenuous ring system. It was discovered in 1977 by James L. Elliot, Edward W. Dunham, and Douglas J. Mink.
  • Uranus has 27 known moons. The largest are TITANIA and OBERON. Their names are those of the queen and king of the fairies in Shakespeare's play "A Midsummer Night's Dream."
  • Each of its moons has its own orbit and characteristics unique.
