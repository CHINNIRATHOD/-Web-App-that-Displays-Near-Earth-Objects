THE MOON

The Moon is the only natural satellite of the Earth and the fifth largest largest of the solar system. Its name comes from the Latin "moon" which means "light", in its archaic form it was "leuksna" and later "louksna". The union of "leuks" (light) and the suffix -na means luminous

Since ancient times, the Moon has fascinated humanity and has been object of study and observation by various cultures.

  • The Moon formed about 4.5 billion years ago and has evolved over time due to geological activity and erosion. It is composed mainly of igneous rocks, such as basalt and anorthosite, and also contains elements such as iron, titanium and helium-3.
  • Moon phases and influence on the Earth → The Moon shows different phases as it moves around the Earth. The Full moon occurs when the Moon is on the opposite side of the Earth to the Sun and the new Moon occurs when the Moon is between the Earth and the Sun. It has a series of effects on the Earth, including tides and stabilization of the Earth's axis.
  • The Moon has been the subject of several space missions, including he Apollo program > from the United States and more recent missions from China and others countries.
